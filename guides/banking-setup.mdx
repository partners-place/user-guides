# U.S. Business Banking Setup Guide

Opening a U.S. business bank account is crucial for operating your American entity. This guide covers everything you need to know about the banking setup process through Portal.

## Why U.S. Business Banking Matters

### Key Benefits:
- **Payment Processing**: Accept payments from U.S. customers
- **Credibility**: Establish trust with U.S. partners and vendors
- **Operations**: Pay U.S. expenses and employees
- **Compliance**: Maintain proper financial records
- **Credit Building**: Establish U.S. business credit history

## Pre-Banking Requirements

Before applying for a business bank account, ensure you have:

<Checklist>
- [ ] Delaware entity formation completed
- [ ] EIN (Employer Identification Number) received
- [ ] Business address established
- [ ] Corporate documents ready
- [ ] Founder identification documents
</Checklist>

## Required Documentation

### Corporate Documents:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- EIN Letter from IRS
- Corporate Bylaws or Operating Agreement
- Banking Resolution

### Personal Documents:
- Valid passport
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
- Personal bank reference letter
- Business plan or company deck

<Tip>
Portal prepares a complete banking package with all necessary documents properly formatted for bank requirements.
</Tip>

## Account Types and Features

### Standard Business Checking
- Monthly maintenance fee: $15-50
- Minimum balance: $1,500-5,000
- Free transactions: 200-500/month
- Online banking included

### Premium Business Account
- Higher transaction limits
- Dedicated relationship manager
- Preferential foreign exchange rates
- Priority wire transfer processing

### Additional Services
- Business credit cards
- Merchant services
- ACH origination
- Positive pay fraud protection

## Managing Your U.S. Bank Account

### Online Banking Setup
1. Receive secure credentials via email
2. Set up two-factor authentication
3. Add authorized users
4. Configure alerts and notifications

### Mobile Banking

### Security Best Practices
<Warning>

- Use unique, strong passwords

- Monitor account daily
</Warning>

## International Transfers

### Receiving Funds from Korea
- **Wire Transfer**: 1-3 business days, $15-45 fee
- **SWIFT Code**: Required for international wires
- **Intermediary Banks**: May be needed for some transfers

### Sending Funds to Korea
- **Daily Limits**: Typically $100,000-250,000
- **Documentation**: May require purpose of transfer
- **Exchange Rates**: Compare bank rates vs. third-party services

## Common Banking Operations

### Check Processing
- Order checks through online banking
- Use mobile deposit for received checks
- Maintain check register for accounting

### ACH Transfers
- Set up vendor payments
- Process payroll
- Receive customer payments
- Lower cost than wire transfers

### Credit Card Processing
- Integrate with payment processors
- Link to business checking account
- Monitor for chargebacks

## Building U.S. Credit

### Timeline:
- **Month 1-3**: Establish account history
- **Month 4-6**: Apply for business credit card
- **Month 7-12**: Build payment history
- **Year 2+**: Access to business loans

### Credit Building Tips:
- Pay all bills on time
- Keep credit utilization low
- Maintain consistent cash flow
- Build relationships with your bank

## Compliance and Reporting

### Monthly Requirements:
- Reconcile bank statements
- Categorize transactions
- Monitor for unusual activity
- Maintain documentation

### Annual Requirements:
- Provide statements for tax filing
- Update banking resolutions
- Review authorized signers
- Confirm account information

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Account Opening Delays">
    Banks may require additional documentation for foreign-owned businesses. Portal helps expedite by providing comprehensive packages upfront.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Transaction Limits">
    New accounts often have lower limits initially. These increase after 3-6 months of normal activity.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="International Wire Issues">
    Ensure all SWIFT codes and intermediary bank information is correct. Portal provides templates for common transfers.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Online Access Problems">
    Some features may be restricted outside the U.S. Use VPN or contact bank for international access options.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
