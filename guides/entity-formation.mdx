# U.S. Entity Formation Guide

This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire process of establishing your U.S. business entity through Portal.

## Pre-Formation Checklist

Before starting the formation process, gather the following information:

<Checklist>
- [ ] Company name (with 2-3 alternatives)
- [ ] Business description and primary activities
- [ ] Founder information (names, addresses, passport copies)
- [ ] Initial shareholding structure
- [ ] Registered agent preference
- [ ] Business address requirements
</Checklist>

## Step 1: Choose Your Entity Type

### C-Corporation vs LLC Comparison

| Feature | C-Corporation | LLC |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| **Best For** | VC funding, going public | Flexibility, small teams |
| **Taxation** | Double taxation | Pass-through taxation |
| **Ownership** | Unlimited shareholders | Members (owners) |
| **Management** | Board of Directors | Member-managed or Manager-managed |
| **Investor Friendly** | Yes (preferred by VCs) | Less attractive to investors |
| **Compliance** | More requirements | Fewer formalities |

<Tip>
Most Korean tech startups planning to raise capital choose C-Corporation. Consult with Portal advisors for personalized recommendations.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Name Your Company

### Naming Requirements
- Must be unique in Delaware
- Cannot use restricted words without approval
- Must include entity identifier (Inc., LLC, etc.)

### Name Search Process
1. Portal checks name availability
2. We suggest alternatives if needed
3. Reserve name for 120 days (optional)

<Warning>
Avoid names too similar to established brands to prevent trademark issues.
</Warning>

## Step 3: Complete Onboarding

### Information Required

**For C-Corporation:**
- Number of authorized shares (typically 10 million)
- Par value (typically $0.0001)
- Board of Directors names
- Officer positions (CEO, Secretary, etc.)

**For LLC:**
- Operating agreement preferences
- Member ownership percentages
- Management structure
- Tax classification election

## Step 4: Document Preparation

Portal prepares and files:

### Formation Documents
- Certificate of Incorporation (C-Corp) or Formation (LLC)
- Corporate Bylaws or Operating Agreement
- Initial Board Resolutions
- Stock Certificates or Membership Certificates

### Post-Formation Documents
- EIN application (Form SS-4)
- Initial Statement of Information
- Foreign qualification documents (if needed)

## Step 5: EIN Application

The Employer Identification Number (EIN) process:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Form Preparation">
    Portal completes IRS Form SS-4 with your information
  </Step>
  <Step title="IRS Submission">
    We submit electronically for fastest processing
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow-up">
    Track application status and handle any IRS queries
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivery">
    Receive EIN letter typically within 1-2 weeks
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Inadequate Shareholding Structure">
    Plan for future funding rounds and employee equity. Standard is 10 million authorized shares for C-Corps.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Mixing Personal and Business">
    Maintain corporate formalities to preserve limited liability protection.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Ignoring Compliance">
    Missing Delaware deadlines can result in penalties and loss of good standing.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Wrong Entity Choice">
    Converting between entity types later is costly and complex.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
